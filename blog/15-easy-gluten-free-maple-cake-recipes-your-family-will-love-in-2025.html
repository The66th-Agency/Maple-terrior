Classic Gluten-Free Maple Layer Cake

Image Source: Dr. Rachel Pauls

This four-layer maple cake blends almond and oat flour perfectly to create a light, fluffy texture that tastes like in traditional cake [1].

Bakers can use our organic pure maple sugar in the recipes that call for granulated maple, the same one we use in our own kitchen.

Ingredients Overview

The foundations of this gluten-free maple cake depend on a precise mix of 1⅓ cup fine almond flour and ⅓ cup plus 3 tablespoons oat flour [1]. The recipe uses both butter and oil to achieve the best moisture retention. Pure maple syrup and coconut sugar blend together to create a naturally sweet flavor without refined sugars [1].

Step-by-Step Instructions

Preheat your oven to 350°F. Whisk together melted butter, eggs, maple syrup, and coconut sugar until smooth. Add the flour mixture and fold it in until you get a pudding-like batter [1]. The batter goes into prepared cake pans and needs about 25 minutes in the oven. A toothpick inserted in the center should come out clean [1].

Maple Buttercream Recipe

Making perfect maple buttercream frosting needs careful attention. Mix butter with pure maple syrup, and make sure you use grade A or B maple syrup with a deep amber color that gives the strongest flavor [1]. Beat the mixture at high speed for 5 minutes. Add powdered sugar slowly until you get a smooth, spreadable texture [1].

Storage Tips

Keep the assembled cake in an airtight container in your fridge and it will stay fresh up to 4 days [2]. You can wrap individual slices in plastic wrap and put them in a freezer-safe container for longer storage [2]. Let the cake reach room temperature before serving to get the best taste and texture.

Room temperature ingredients and gentle mixing are crucial for success. The cake layers must cool completely before you add the maple buttercream or it will melt [1]. This classic maple layer cake balances moisture and flavor perfectly, making it great for special occasions or weekend treats.

One-Bowl Maple Almond Cake

Image Source: Munchyesta

This one-bowl maple almond cake brings together simplicity and elegance to create amazing flavors without any fuss. The recipe shines with its balanced combination of almond flour and natural maple sweetness.

5-Minute Prep Method

Your oven needs to be preheated to 350°F while you line an 8-inch cake pan with parchment paper [3]. A single bowl works great to mix 2 cups of firmly packed almond flour with baking powder and baking soda [3]. The next step combines 4 eggs, ⅔ cup maple syrup, and ¼ cup extra-virgin olive oil until they blend really well [3]. Just fold the wet ingredients into the dry mixture until everything comes together.

Perfect Texture Tips

The batter needs 30 minutes of rest time before baking [4]. This vital step helps the almond flour absorb all liquid ingredients completely. Your cake will have a tender crumb without any grittiness [4]. Look for a smooth, pudding-like consistency in your batter [5].

A timer works better than just watching the cake. Set it for exactly 40-45 minutes [3]. Gluten-free cakes might look underdone even when they're ready [4]. A toothpick inserted into the center should come out clean to confirm it's done [3].

Almond Maple Glaze

A luxurious almond maple glaze makes this cake special. Mix 4 tablespoons of creamy almond butter with 3 tablespoons non-dairy milk and 2 tablespoons maple syrup [6]. One tablespoon of tapioca flour whisked in creates the perfect consistency [6].

The cake should be slightly warm when you add the glaze [3]. This way, the glaze seeps into the cake and creates an amazing moist texture. Sliced almonds on top add a nice crunch and make it look beautiful [3].

The cake stays fresh in an airtight container in your refrigerator for up to 4 days [3]. You'll notice the flavors taste even better the next day after they've had time to blend [7]. Each slice freezes well for up to 3 months if you need longer storage [7].

Vegan Maple Oat Flour Cake

Image Source: The Conscious Plant Kitchen

Let's revolutionize your maple cake recipe by making it vegan with oat flour. This gluten-free alternative creates tender, moist baked goods and adds a subtle nutty flavor that everyone loves.

Egg Replacement Guide

The right binding agents make all the difference in egg-free maple cakes. You'll get the best results when you mix 3 tablespoons chickpea flour with 3 tablespoons water to replace each egg [8]. Another great option is aquafaba (chickpea liquid) - 3 tablespoons equals one whole egg [9]. The binding properties get a nice boost when you lightly whip the aquafaba for 45-60 seconds until it turns foamy [9].

Oat Flour Benefits

Oat flour really shines among gluten-free alternatives. The commercial version comes from milled oat groats and creates remarkably light and tender cakes thanks to its perfect balance of starch, fat, and protein [10]. Oat flour's high fiber content and viscous texture work like gluten to bind ingredients together [11].

Vegan Frosting Options

These dairy-free frosting alternatives will take your vegan maple cake to the next level:

  1. Maple Cashew Frosting: Start by soaking 1½ cups raw cashews in boiling water for an hour. Blend them with ¼ cup maple syrup, 3 tablespoons almond milk, and 1 teaspoon vanilla extract until smooth [1]. This creates a deliciously creamy, naturally sweetened topping.

  2. Vegan Maple Buttercream: Mix 2 cups non-hydrogenated vegetable shortening with ¼ cup plus 2 tablespoons maple syrup and 1 tablespoon maple extract [1]. Beat the mixture until it turns light and fluffy for that classic buttercream texture.

Room temperature ingredients will give you the best results [1]. Your finished cake stays fresh in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days [1]. Pop it in the fridge to keep it fresh longer, but let it warm up a bit before serving.

No-Sugar-Added Maple Apple Cake

Image Source: Turning gluten-free, one recipe at a time

This maple apple cake skips refined sugar but keeps all the delicious sweetness you love. You'll love how pure maple syrup and fresh apples come together to create this wholesome treat.

Natural Sweetening Tips

Pure maple syrup works as the main sweetener in this recipe. Dark amber varieties (grade A or B) give you the best flavor [12]. The recipe becomes even better when you combine maple syrup with unsweetened applesauce. This adds moisture and natural sweetness [13]. Just ½ cup plus 1 tablespoon maple syrup creates the perfect sweetness without any refined sugar [14].

Apple Selection Guide

The recipe needs firm, tart apples that hold up well during baking. Granny Smith apples give you the ideal tartness. Honeycrisp, Pink Lady, or Fuji apples work great too [15]. Slice your peeled apples about ¼-inch thick so they bake evenly [15]. A quick splash of lemon juice on the apple slices prevents browning [14].

Serving Suggestions

This versatile cake tastes amazing in several ways:

  • A warm slice with coconut whipped cream makes a perfect dairy-free treat [15]

  • Hot apple cider pairs beautifully with this autumn favorite [13]

  • A drizzle of maple-apple glaze made from concentrated apple cider adds extra flavor [15]

Your cake stays moist for 4 days in an airtight container [15]. Keep it at room temperature the first day, then move it to the fridge [16]. The flavors get even better overnight, which makes this cake perfect to bake ahead [13].

The secret lies in mixing the batter just enough to get a tender crumb [15]. Bake at 350°F for about 30-32 minutes until a toothpick comes out clean [15]. The maple syrup and apples create magic in the oven. Their natural sugars caramelize into a golden crust while keeping the inside incredibly moist [14].

Maple Walnut Bundt Cake

Image Source: Paleo Gluten-Free Guy

You just need meticulous attention to detail to bake a perfect maple walnut bundt cake. The process starts from pan preparation and continues to the final decorative touches. This classic recipe combines rich maple flavor with crunchy walnuts to create an irresistible gluten-free treat.

Pan Preparation

The right pan preparation guides you to success. Pure oil spray or melted shortening works better than traditional butter to coat every crevice really well [17]. Dust the pan with granulated sugar or cocoa powder right after, since regular flour often makes the cake stick [17]. Your best results come when you prepare the pan right before adding the batter, which prevents oil from pooling at the bottom [17].

Walnut Topping

Toast the nuts in a medium frying pan over medium-high heat for about 5 minutes to create a delectable walnut topping [18]. Keep stirring to ensure even browning and prevent burning. Coarsely chop the cooled walnuts to maintain texture [19]. You can raise the presentation by mixing ½ cup toasted walnuts with ¼ cup maple syrup and ¾ teaspoon cinnamon into a maple-walnut filling [19].

Baking Time Guide

Temperature control and timing help you achieve perfect doneness. Preheat the oven to 350°F, then reduce to 325°F to ensure even baking [19]. The cake should bake for 50-55 minutes until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean [20]. Let it cool in the pan for exactly 10 minutes [20].

Mix ⅔ cup coconut cream with 2 teaspoons maple syrup and ¾ teaspoon vanilla extract to make a professional maple glaze [19]. Pour this glaze over the completely cooled cake and let it drip down the sides naturally. Sprinkle additional toasted walnuts on top right away for garnish.

The cake stays fresh in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 5 days [19]. You can also wrap individual slices in plastic wrap and freeze them for up to 3 months. Just thaw them overnight in the refrigerator when needed [2].

5-Ingredient Maple Mug Cake

Image Source: Dr. Rachel Pauls

Want a quick maple treat? This five-ingredient mug cake makes the perfect single-serving dessert in under two minutes. Simple pantry staples come together to create a soft, tender cake that hits your sweet tooth just right.

Microwave Instructions

Add one large egg, 2 tablespoons maple syrup, and 1 tablespoon almond milk. Stir until the mixture becomes smooth [22].

Pop it in the microwave at full power for 90-100 seconds [5]. You'll know it's done when the cake pulls away from the mug's sides [21]. Let it rest for 1-2 minutes since it keeps cooking a bit after you take it out [22].

Quick Toppings

Room temperature ingredients work best [25]. The cake tastes amazing right away and stays moist when covered at room temperature for 1-2 days [26]. You can also mix the dry ingredients ahead and store them separately to save time [5].

The butter mixture should stay soft but not completely melted [3]. Drop spoonfuls of this mixture over your cake batter with enough space between each dollop [3].

Take a butter knife and swirl the cinnamon mixture through the top layer of batter gently. Don't press too deep or the filling will sink [3]. Make generous loops without over-mixing to keep distinct ribbons of cinnamon throughout the cake.

Maple Glaze Recipe

A maple glaze lifts this cake from good to extraordinary. Mix 2 cups confectioners' sugar with 2 tablespoons pure maple syrup and 5 tablespoons milk [28]. Add ¼ teaspoon maple extract to boost the maple flavor [29]. Whisk everything until you get a smooth, drizzling consistency. Put the glaze on while the cake is still slightly warm so it seeps into the surface [29].

Make-Ahead Tips

You can make this maple cake up to two days ahead. Keep the unglazed cake in an airtight container at room temperature [30]. The cake freezes well too - wrap individual slices in plastic wrap for up to 2-3 months [31]. Let frozen slices thaw overnight in your refrigerator and warm them briefly before serving [31].

The batter timing matters - bake at 350°F for 25-30 minutes until a toothpick comes out clean from the cake part [27]. The cake develops a slightly craggy top as it cools, just like traditional cinnamon rolls [3]. This creates a texture that pairs perfectly with the maple-cinnamon flavors.
