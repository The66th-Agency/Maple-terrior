Local Maple and Quebec

Boston Is Surrounded by Maple. Here Is Why Quebec Single-Origin Still Has a Place.

An honest comparison, with no put-down of American maple.

New England maple is some of the best in the world, and Vermont in particular is a benchmark US producer. A jug of Vermont or western Massachusetts syrup from a good farm needs no defending, and Boston buyers are spoiled for honest local choice.

Single-origin Quebec is a parallel option, not a replacement. The Appalachian belt that gives Vermont its syrup runs north across the Quebec border, where colder winters concentrate the sap and the premium tier is sharply competitive because Quebec makes most of the world supply.

Maple Terroir comes from one family farm in that belt, triple-certified organic and carrying the USDA Organic seal. For a Boston kitchen, it sits alongside the Vermont jug as a single-source, traceable bottle from the same mountain range, just over the line in Canada.
