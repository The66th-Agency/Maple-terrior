Local Maple and Quebec

New York Has Its Own Maple. Here Is Why New Yorkers Still Buy Quebec.

An honest comparison, with no put-down of American maple.

New York State produces a serious share of US maple, and the Upstate tradition is real and worth supporting. A syrup from a good Adirondack or Finger Lakes farm is a fine bottle, and the state's maple weekends draw crowds every March.

The premium tier in New York City food retail tends to concentrate on single-origin syrup for the same reason wine buyers concentrate on a single estate: the producer is transparent, the certification is denser, and the flavour traces to one piece of land. Quebec produces the majority of the world supply, so its premium tier is sharply competitive.

Maple Terroir sits in that tier, single-origin from one Appalachian farm and carrying the USDA Organic seal New York shoppers already recognise. None of which takes anything away from the maple families upstate. The two categories serve different buyers, and many New York kitchens keep both.
