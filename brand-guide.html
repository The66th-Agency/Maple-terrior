01

Brand Overview

Who We Are

Fourth-generation, family-owned maple company from Quebec's Appalachian Mountains.

Founded in 1978. Single-origin. Certified organic. Distributed globally. The company combines deep agricultural heritage with premium editorial positioning.

Positioning

Maple Terroir sits in the same tier as Aesop, Le Labo, and other premium artisan brands. Considered, warm, and quietly confident.

The product speaks for itself. The brand creates the right atmosphere around it — never corporate, never rustic-kitschy.

Voice and Tone

Direct and warm. Storytelling over selling. The language should feel like it comes from someone who has been making maple syrup for four generations — not a marketer.

  • —Lead with place and process, not features and claims
  • —Quebec Appalachian Mountains is a specific identity, not just a location
  • —"Single-origin" and "fourth-generation" are the two most important descriptors

03

Photography Style

Lighting

Soft natural light, always warm. Golden hour warmth. No harsh shadows, no ring lights, no cool blue tones.

Mood

Premium editorial food photography. Kinfolk and Bon Appétit, not Instagram flat lay. Warm kitchen or Quebec forest at golden hour.

Texture Props

Linen, raw maple wood, maple bark, parchment, brushed brass. These are the only supporting textures.

Do

  • +Warm, soft natural lighting
  • +Shallow depth of field, soft bokeh backgrounds
  • +Linen and wood surfaces
  • +Intentionally imperfect, organic compositions
  • +Amber and cream colour grading throughout
  • +Documentary feel for heritage/landscape shots

Don't

  • -Sterile white or studio-lit backgrounds
  • -Blue or purple colour casts — ever
  • -Glossy reflections or plastic-looking surfaces
  • -Oversaturated colours
  • -Stock photo compositions or grid flat lays
  • -Branded packaging as the hero element

Mist in the distance. Feels cold but beautiful — not sterile or blue. Warm white snow tones, not blue-gray. Cinematic landscape photography.
